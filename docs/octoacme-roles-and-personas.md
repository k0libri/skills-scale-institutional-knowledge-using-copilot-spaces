# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the end-to-end release process, coordinating with QA, developers, and stakeholders to ensure smooth, predictable deployments. They own release schedules, readiness criteria, and sign-off gates.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules across teams
- Oversee deployment readiness and pre-release checklists
- Ensure stakeholder sign-off before production releases
- Coordinate with QA to validate release quality gates
- Manage release notes and communicate release status
- Own rollback decisions and coordinate incident response during deployments

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Maintain clear release cadence and stakeholder visibility

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with QA and delivery leads
- Go/no-go sign-off meetings with stakeholders
- Release announcements and post-deployment status updates
- Coordination with Project Communications Lead for stakeholder updates

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinates feature freeze, merge windows, and deployment timing
- **Product Managers**: Aligns release content with product priorities and customer commitments
- **Project Managers**: Syncs on project timelines and resource availability
- **QA**: Partners on release quality gates and smoke test execution
- **Project Communications Lead**: Collaborates on release announcements and stakeholder notifications

---

## Project Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Project Communications Leads ensure clear, consistent communication across all project stakeholders. They manage internal and external updates, meeting logistics, and ensure transparency throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate effective communication among teams and stakeholders
- Manage internal and external project status updates
- Ensure stakeholders are aligned on project progress and milestones
- Coordinate meeting logistics and agendas
- Maintain communication templates and channels
- Escalate communication gaps or misalignments to Project Managers

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders have timely, accurate project information
- Reduce communication overhead and miscommunication
- Maintain transparency and trust across project teams

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder updates and milestone briefings
- Meeting facilitation and agenda management
- Communication templates (status updates, announcements)
- Cross-team coordination and sync meetings

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on communication plans and escalation paths
- **Product Managers**: Coordinates roadmap and milestone communications
- **Release Manager**: Collaborates on release announcements and deployment notifications
- **Developers**: Gathers technical updates for stakeholder reporting
- **Risk Owner**: Communicates risk status and mitigation updates to stakeholders

---

## Risk Owner

### Role Summary
Risk Owners identify, monitor, and manage significant project risks. They maintain the risk register, develop mitigation strategies, and ensure risks are communicated to leadership and relevant stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and document major project risks
- Assess risk impact and likelihood
- Develop and track mitigation strategies
- Maintain and update the risk register
- Escalate critical risks to leadership and stakeholders
- Collaborate with teams to implement risk mitigations
- Report risk status in regular project reviews

### Goals
- Proactively identify and address risks before they become issues
- Minimize project disruptions from unmanaged risks
- Maintain visibility and accountability for risk management

### Typical Communication
- Risk register updates and review meetings
- Escalation communications to leadership
- Mitigation planning sessions with affected teams
- Risk status updates in weekly project syncs

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partners on risk identification and project timeline impacts
- **Developers**: Collaborates on technical risk assessment and mitigation
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on product-related risks and trade-offs
- **Release Manager**: Coordinates on release-related risks and go/no-go decisions
- **Project Communications Lead**: Ensures risk status is communicated to stakeholders
